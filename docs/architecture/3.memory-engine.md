# Cliper Memory Engine

> "What if your repository had a brain?"

---

## The Problem

Large language models are stateless.

Every conversation begins with something like:

> "Here's my repository..."

The AI forgets everything after the conversation ends.

The next day...

You repeat yourself.

Again.

And again.

---

## A Real World Analogy

Imagine hiring a senior software engineer.

On their first day they read the documentation,
explore the repository,
understand the architecture,
learn the release history,
and gradually become productive.

Now imagine...

Every morning they wake up with complete amnesia.

You spend two hours every day explaining:

- how the project works
- where authentication lives
- why that module exists
- which release introduced a feature

That's exactly how today's AI assistants behave.

Cliper exists to solve that.

---

# The Idea

Instead of repeatedly giving AI the repository,

we teach it once.

Then store what it learned as structured memories.

Those memories become searchable forever.
